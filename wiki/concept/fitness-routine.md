## Overview

Ford has an active but inconsistent fitness life. He describes it as one of the most "disjointed" areas of his life and has a strong desire to improve it. He is 37 years old and increasingly aware of the need to invest in his physical health.

## Current Routine

**Gym:** Gold's Gym (Richmond, VA)
**Frequency:** Aims for daily; doesn't always hit it.
**Timing:** After dropping kids off at school on co-parenting days.
**Session (~1 hour):**
- Jump rope
- Heavy bag work
- Shadow boxing
- Light yoga
- Treadmill running
- 15-minute sauna

## Running

- Running is a genuine passion.
- Recently lost weight and is running better than in the past few years.
- Loves trail running in Richmond specifically.
- Wants to run significantly more.

## Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu

- Favorite sport overall.
- Current rank: **white belt, 0 stripes**.
- Recurring pattern: starts a season → gets interrupted by life/cost → stops → has to relearn from scratch.
- Wants to commit more seriously when life settles down.
- Cost is a barrier.

## Weightlifting (Aspirational)

- Wants to add a heavy-weight lifting routine.
- Feels uncomfortable starting — barrier is psychological, not logistical (currently).
- Cannot set up home weights because he is living with his parents and lacks his own space.

## Constraints & Context

- **Co-parenting schedule**: kids half the week, which creates variability in routine.
- **Living with parents**: no space for home gym equipment.
- **Busy life**: BJJ and other fitness goals repeatedly deprioritized by work, family, and transitions.

## Goals

- More consistent daily exercise habit.
- Build a structured strength/weight training program.
- Return to BJJ with sustained commitment.
- Run more, especially on Richmond trails.
- Take meaningfully better care of his body long-term.
