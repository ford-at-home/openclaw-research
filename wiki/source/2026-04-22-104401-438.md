## Summary

Ford describes what he values in a neighborhood: residents who are energetic and healthy, people with resources who invest in community reciprocity, excellent public schools (elementary through high school), access to green space, and a safe environment where kids can roam freely. He anchors the search firmly in Richmond, preferring the West End or South Side.

## Key Ideas

- **Community energy matters**: Ford wants neighbors who are vibrant and healthy, not depressed — this is a quality-of-life filter, not just a practical one.
- **Reciprocal community**: He explicitly wants people who "take care of me just as much as I take care of them" — mutual investment is a core criterion.
- **Schools are non-negotiable**: Strong public schools at all three levels (elementary, middle, high) are described as "a huge one."
- **Green space access**: Parks or natural areas are a stated requirement.
- **Free-range kids**: Safety and community trust sufficient to let children roam independently.
- **Geography**: Richmond is the only acceptable city; West End and South Side are the preferred quadrants.
